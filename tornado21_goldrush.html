Tornado 21 — The Goldrush

A multi-stage technique that turns one Lesser Straight into an unending chain of bonuses.

What is it

A goldrush is a repeatable play sequence that keeps re-triggering a Lesser Straight bonus, cycle after cycle. Each cycle pours fresh gold cards and scorebox gaps into the game, so subsequent clears score for double, quadruple, and beyond. A well-run goldrush lifts a score that would normally finish in the thousands into the millions.

Mechanically it’s a three-stage routine: set up a specific board shape (a soft 20 alongside a soft gold 16), then loop a short sequence of plays that rebuilds the same lesser every time. The loop only breaks when the deck deals an unexpected joker.

Why it works

A Lesser Straight (column sums 16, 17, 18, 19, 20) instantly adds 5 spade gold cards (A–5) to the deck and opens 5 scorebox gaps. Instead of clearing the board with them, a goldrush piles them into a single column: that column doubles per gold, the gaps double the next clears, and you earn the All Golden multiplier on the resulting bonus. Each rebuilt lesser adds more golds and more gaps — the loop snowballs.

The goal of the setup is a very specific lesser: one with a soft 20 and a soft gold 16. Once you reach that state, the goldrush loop (Stage 3) runs almost mechanically.

Soft vs. hard — what makes the goldrush possible A column is soft while it contains an Ace and its low total is 11 or less. The Ace counts as either 1 or 11, so a soft column reads as two numbers (e.g. “10 or 20” for a soft 20, “6 or 16” for a soft 16). The moment another card pushes the low total past 11, the Ace locks to 1 and the column becomes hard.

Why it matters: a hard 20 is dead weight — the only card it can accept without busting is an Ace. A soft 20 is dual-use: it counts as 21 for clearing purposes, but you can also play onto it (using the low “10” reading). For example, playing a 5 onto a 4-card soft 20 makes the column read 15 and become hard — but it now holds 5 cards, and a full column clears regardless of total. The goldrush loop depends on this constantly: soft columns get cleared, reused, and rebuilt without you ever needing to land exactly on 21.

A gold soft column is the same thing built (partly or entirely) from gold cards. Two things change compared to a white soft column: (1) its eventual clear scores doubled per gold, and (2) it feeds the joker engine — any clear-bonus on a column with 2 or more gold cards spawns a joker (except Triple 7’s and Double Jokers), and playing onto a column that already holds 3 or more golds also spawns one. The goldrush loop relies on those jokers showing up exactly when the routine calls for them.

Stage 1 — Lesser Straight with a hard 20 → build a soft 20

You’ve hit a Lesser Straight (column totals 16/17/18/19/20), but your 20 is hard — no Ace, so it can’t be played onto. You need to replace it with a soft 20 before continuing. Skip this stage if your 20 is already soft.

The lesser awards 5+4+3+2+Ace. You need a free column to pile 4+3+2+Ace and you need to dispose of the 5 first.

  1. Play the 5 on the 16 (clears it).
  2. Pile 4+3+2+Ace into the freed column.
  3. While piling, jokers will arrive — park every joker on the column holding the old 20, clearing it. The new soft 20 is being built in the pile.
  4. Rebuild the lesser (you need a new 16) without touching the new soft 20. Often fails; retry.
Most goldrushers avoid Stage 1 by building the soft 20 before the lesser, so they only have to rebuild the lesser once (in Stage 2). A soft white 20 on the first lesser is the ideal start.

Stage 2 — Lesser Straight with a soft 20 → build a soft gold 16

You have a Lesser Straight (16/17/18/19/20) with a soft 20 (gold or white — either works). Now turn the 16 into a soft gold 16 built from the incoming 3+2+Ace.
  1. Play the 5 on the 16 (clears it). The soft 20 stays put — never sacrifice it.
  2. Play the 4 on the 17. You now have two free columns.
  3. Stack 3+2+Ace in one free column (your new soft gold 16).
  4. Park every joker that arrives in the other free column.
  5. Rebuild the lesser (a new 17) without touching the 16 or 20. If forced, sacrifice the 16 and redo Stage 2 only.
When this succeeds you have the goldrush lesser: soft 20 + soft gold 16. Proceed to Stage 3.

Stage 3 — Goldrushing

Loop a fresh lesser out of the current one every cycle, harvesting golds and gaps each pass.

Which sub-routine to run depends on the composition of your soft 20:

Case 1 — 4-card gold soft 20 You arrive here from Stage 1, or after looping from any other case.
  • 5 onto the soft 20 → 5-card clear → joker.
  • Joker into the freshly cleared column.
  • 4 onto the 16 → new soft 20 → joker.
  • Joker onto the parked joker → clears.
  • Stack 3+2+Ace in the cleared column.
Result: a fresh goldrush lesser. Repeat until you receive an unexpected joker — the Extra Joker — which breaks the loop.
Case 2 — Soft white 20 (2–3 white cards)
  • Gold 5 on the 20.
  • Gold 4 on the gold 16.
  • Clear the resulting 15 with the joker.
  • Stack 3+2+Ace in the empty column.
  • Loop into Case 1.
Case 3 — 2–3 card gold soft 20
  • Gold 5 on the soft 20.
  • Joker on the gold 15.
  • Next joker into the freshly cleared column.
  • Gold 4 on the gold 16.
  • Joker onto the parked joker.
  • Stack 3+2+Ace in the empty column.
  • Loop into Case 1.
Case 4 — Rare: 4-white soft 20 + 2-white soft 16
  • Gold 5 on the soft 20 → clears, no joker comes.
  • 4 on the soft 16.
  • Stack 3+2+Ace in the empty column.
  • Loop into Case 2.

Endgame

The loop ends when an extra joker arrives that none of the routines accounted for. Until then, every cycle adds more golds to the deck and more gaps to the scorebox, doubling the next clears compounding-style. Top scores in the original game reached the tens of millions using this and the related Meta-rush follow-up.
